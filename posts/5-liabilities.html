5 Liabilities

Liabilities are the opposite side of the balance sheet from Assets. Just as Savings are an Asset to the person receiving income from it, Savings are a Liability for the person or people paying that income.

Conclusions

Looked at more generally, for our three types of Asset:

  • Currency is only ever a liability of the Bank of England, as they are the only ones that can produce it

  • Equity and Debt are liabilities of whoever is paying the Dividends or Interest. On first look, this is the company who issued the equity or debt. But the company is just a middle man - ultimately the dividends and interest are paid by consumers who buy products from the company.

Considered across the whole of society, Savings are a Liability for everyone.

This is a great example of how our model differs from the real world by design. Even though the customers of a company are benefiting from the arrangement through the products they buy, in our financial model we only see the downside from their perspective - the money they must pay.
