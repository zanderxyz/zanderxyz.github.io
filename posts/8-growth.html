8 Growth

Assuming no new money is created from one year to the next, the following year’s GNI will depend on:

  • What proportion of last year’s income is spent, and how many times
  • What proportion of existing Savings are spent

$GNI_{n+1} = m GNI_n + m_s c S_n$

where:

  • $GNI_n$ is the level of GNI in year n
  • $m$ is the Structural Multiplier, the Multiplier on $GNI_n$
  • $S_n$ is the total of existing Savings in year n
  • $c$ is the proportion of existing Savings that are spent each year
  • $m_s$ is the Multiplier on the spent Savings

GNI from year to year can therefore only be increased by:

  • Increasing the Multipliers
  • Encouraging existing Savings to be spent
  • Creating and spending new money

In order for GNI to grow by a rate comfortably above 0%, we need the Structural Multiplier to be close to or above 100%. If it isn’t, then new money will have to be created and spent each year in order to grow GNI enough to avoid a recession.
