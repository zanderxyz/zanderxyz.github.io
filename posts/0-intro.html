Introduction

Among many of those who have studied a lot of economics or finance, there’s often a sense that economics quantifies and explains the real world. It’s a tempting viewpoint, as it lets decision makers replace subjective judgements with models, and point to numbers thrown out of them as “proof” that what they’re doing is right.

The problem is simple: it gives a completely false sense of understanding.

Conventional macroeconomic models start off with a series of assumptions (individual agents, rational behaviour, ignoring stock of financial assets, ..) that simplify the calculations, but lead to a house of cards that falls apart if you look at it too closely.

At their best, models can give us a way to project the incredibly complex real world into a simpler space with fewer variables. Finance is a huge driver behind growth in the real economy, and recessions in the real world are caused when mistakes in the financial world intrude on real life.

I present here a very simple alternative model of the financial world, and explain how we can use it to build an economy that is self-sustaining.

There will be no attempts here to quantify non-financial benefits or costs. Many of the best developments in the real world give non-financial benefits but come with financial costs, and limiting the model in this way forces us to pay these costs rather than letting them build up before they cause a crash.
